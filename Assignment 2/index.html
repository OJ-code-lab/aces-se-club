Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterized by feathers, beaks without teeth, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, high metabolic rates, and strong yet lightweight skeletons.


Birds can be found in almost every habitat on Earth, from the icy regions of the Arctic and Antarctic to the tropical rainforests and vast deserts.
